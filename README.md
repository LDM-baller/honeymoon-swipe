# Honeymoon Swipe

A tiny web app that helps a couple figure out where to go on their honeymoon.
Each partner swipes through real, editor-loved places; keeps the ones that spark
something; ranks their favourites; and the app blends both people into a brief
you hand to Claude for a two-week, two-stop itinerary.

## The couple's brief (baked into the deck + the Claude brief)
- **2 weeks, late November.** One stop for week 1, another for week 2.
- **True luxury** — Aman tier.
- **Amazing food.**
- **Light activity only** — easy walks, no real hiking.
- **Off-the-beaten-path / earth experiences a big plus** (she'd love fossil or gem digging).
- Every destination must genuinely be good to visit in **late November**.
- Natural shape: pair an **experiential** week with a **decompress** week.

## How it works
1. **Swipe (Stage 1).** Each partner opens the app, enters their name, picks Partner A/B,
   and uses the *same trip code*. They swipe through the deck (drag or the ♥ / ✕ buttons).
2. **Rank (Stage 2).** Their right-swipes come back as a list to order, favourite-first.
3. **Combine.** On one phone, or by pasting a partner's **share code**, the app shows:
   - **You both loved** — mutual right-swipes (the anchors).
   - **Which curator you trust** — a source hit-rate bar chart (how many likes each
     curator earned). This tells you whose taste to weight in the final pick.
   - **The brief for Claude** — a copy-paste block that turns all of it into an itinerary.

## Key design decision — the cards are *signals*, not the answer
What you swipe on is a real, specific place from a real curator, but each card also
carries hidden **tags** (region, food, pace, remoteness, week-fit…). The right-swipes
sum into a preference profile. The final itinerary is generated by Claude from that
profile + the hard filters — so no one has to have "picked the right place."

## Content & sourcing rules (important)
- Cards are **real places** drawn from real curators (Condé Nast Traveler, Vogue,
  chic travel blogs), each with a `source` + `source_url` so we can measure hit-rate.
- We **do not reproduce** the source's article text or photographs. Card copy is
  original (our words), images are the **property's own press photos or licensed stock**,
  and a **"Featured in … ↗"** link sends the reader to the real article.
- A short **cited quote** (in quotes, in the `quote` field) is fine where a line is essential.

## Data model
See `data/cards.js` — top comment documents every field. Cards are loaded as a plain
script (not fetched JSON) so the app runs when you just open `index.html` from disk.

## Running it
- **Locally:** double-click `index.html`. Everything works offline; picks save to the
  browser's localStorage and combine via share code.
- **Hosting:** it's static, so it drops onto GitHub Pages (public) or any private host.
  Because the cards are attribution-style (no copied articles), public hosting is clean.
  If you ever switch to verbatim source content, keep it off a public URL.
